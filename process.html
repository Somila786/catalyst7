Section · 02 / The operating system

Every engagement runs the same loop, whatever the layer. We ground ourselves in the real business, take a position, build against it, ship it into the market, and judge the whole thing on a single number.

It's deliberately short. No forty-page decks, no quiet pilots, no work that never sees daylight. The point of a process is to remove the places work goes to die — not to add ceremony around them.

Five phases, one accountable lead, one clock. You always know which phase you're in, what comes out of it, and how long it takes.

Section · 03 / The five phases

From first listen to a number on the board. Each phase has a clear output and a fixed window.

01
Intelligence

Listen

A two-week ground-truthing of the business — its people, its numbers and its real bottleneck. We sit with operators, not just the org chart, and find the one constraint actually holding things back.

Deliverable
Diagnostic read & named constraint
Duration
Two weeks
02
Intelligence

Position

A one-page decision document — the thesis the work will defend. We take a side and commit to it on paper before a line of work is built, so everyone is steering toward the same outcome.

Deliverable
One-page decision document
Duration
Days, not weeks
03
Action

Build

Engineering, strategy and production move in parallel on weekly clocks. Every week shows something real — working software, live work, a decision made — never a status report about future work.

Deliverable
Working increments, weekly
Cadence
Weekly proof
04
Action

Ship

In market within ninety days. No quiet pilots, no permanent betas, no work that never sees daylight. If it doesn't run in the real world for real users, we don't call it shipped.

Deliverable
Live work, in market
Window
Within 90 days
05
Measurement

Measure

A single number per engagement, reviewed every quarter. We agree it up front, we report it honestly, and we earn the next quarter — or we don't. One number keeps everyone honest about what the work is for.

Deliverable
One number, reviewed
Cadence
Every quarter
